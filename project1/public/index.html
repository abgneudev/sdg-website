Sustainable Development Goals

The Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs), also known as the Global Goals, were adopted by the United Nations in 2015 as a universal call to action to end poverty, protect the planet, and ensure that by 2030 all people enjoy peace and prosperity.

    Habitat loss in polar region due to global warming

    Since 1980, greenhouse gas emissions doubled, elevating global temperatures by 0.7 degrees Celsius and imperiling ecosystems like coral reefs and polar regions. Conservation of carbon-storing ecosystems is vital, and collaborating with nature could significantly reduce emissions by 2030, a crucial strategy to mitigate global warming.

  • 13th February 2024

    Nature is staging a comeback: UN recognizes seven World Restoration Flagships

    UN Environment Programme (UNEP) and Food and Agriculture Organization of the UN (FAO) have identified seven initiatives across Africa, Latin America, the Mediterranean, and South Asia as UN World Restoration Flagships. These projects target ecosystems on the brink of degradation due to wildfires, drought, deforestation, and pollution, now eligible for UN support.
